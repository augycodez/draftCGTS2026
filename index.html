About the Symposium.

Now in its 11th year, the Cell and Gene Therapy Symposium has become a leading forum for CGT professionals across India and internationally. Since its launch in 2015 with just 80 participants, the symposium has evolved into a flagship event that attracts global leaders and innovators in viral vectors, CAR-T therapies, gene editing, and advanced manufacturing.

Hands-on Workshop on CAR-T Cell Engineering and Characterization by Flow Cytometry.

Coordinators: Dr. Thiyagaraj Mayuranathan & Dr. Sandya Rani

Overview

This hands-on workshop provides a practical introduction to T-cell processing workflows relevant to immune cell therapy including CAR-T cell therapy. Participants will gain hands on experience on T-cell isolation, activation using CD3/CD28 Dynabeads, and phenotypic characterization by flow cytometry. Through a combination of live demonstrations, guided hands-on exercises, and pre-prepared samples, attendees will observe key steps in T cell enrichment, activation, transduction and expansion. The workshop will also introduce the fundamentals of genetic modification, with a demonstration of CD19 CAR transduction in T cells. Emphasis will be placed on T cell processing, modification, staining panel design, instrument operation, and data analysis strategies. Each step is contextualized within the CAR-T manufacturing pipeline, providing participants with both practical skills and translational insight.

Who Should Attend

  • PhD scholars & Post Doctoral Fellows
  • Academic researchers in life sciences
  • Industry professionals in Cell & Gene Therapy & Biotechnology

Unique Features

  • Extensive hands-on training
  • Small-group guided sessions
  • Industry-standard data pipelines

Learning Outcomes

  • Gain hands-on expertise in ddPCR workflows
  • Perform cell isolation using industry-standard methods
  • Understand the basic concepts of flow cytometry, panel design, sample acquisition, gating strategies and data analysis.
  • Understand clinical and translational applications

Note

  • Seats are limited to 30 participants to ensure a high-quality, hands-on experience.
  • Standalone registration for the workshop is not permitted; participants must register for both the workshop and the symposium.
  • Late or spot registration is not available.
