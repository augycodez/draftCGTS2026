About the Symposium

A Decade of Progress.
A Future of Cures.

Now in its 11th year, the Cell and Gene Therapy Symposium has become a leading forum for CGT professionals across India and internationally. Since its launch in 2015 with just 80 participants, the symposium has evolved into a flagship event that attracts global leaders and innovators in viral vectors, CAR-T therapies, gene editing, and advanced manufacturing.

Our mission is to fast-track the journey from groundbreaking scientific discovery to transformative therapies for patients with rare and complex diseases. Through high-impact scientific sessions, interactive workshops, and purposeful networking opportunities, CGTS 2026 will further advance the frontiers of curative medicine.

Scientific Programme

Core Themes 2026

This year's symposium brings together fourteen focus areas spanning clinical translation, disease biology, platform innovation, and real-world manufacturing insights in cell and gene therapy.

Hematological Disorders 1 - Hemoglobinopathies

Sessions will spotlight translational advances for sickle cell disease and thalassemia, where cell and gene therapy are reshaping standards of care. The discussion fits the symposium's focus on moving durable therapies from bench to bedside.

Hematological Disorders 2 - Bleeding Disorders, Primary Immunodeficiency Disorders

This theme connects curative strategies for coagulation defects and immune dysfunction with emerging gene correction platforms. It creates a strong clinical bridge between rare disease biology and therapeutic innovation at the symposium.

Immune Cell Therapy

Immune cell therapy remains one of the most exciting frontiers for engineered cellular medicines across cancer and beyond. This track will highlight platform advances, clinical lessons, and future directions highly relevant to translational researchers and clinicians.

Ophthalmological Disorders

The eye continues to be a powerful setting for targeted gene and cell-based interventions with measurable clinical outcomes. Symposium discussions in this area will emphasize precision delivery, durability, and disease-specific translation.

Neurological Disorders

Neurological diseases present some of the most urgent unmet needs and some of the toughest delivery challenges in the field. This theme will bring together insights on CNS targeting, safety, and long-term functional outcomes.

Cell Therapy for Diabetes

Beta-cell replacement and stem cell-derived approaches are rapidly redefining what disease modification can mean in diabetes. This symposium theme is especially relevant for connecting regenerative biology with clinically scalable therapy design.

Manufacturing And Regulatory Aspects in Cell & Gene Therapy

Successful translation depends not only on discovery, but also on robust manufacturing, quality systems, and regulatory alignment. This track will anchor the symposium in the practical realities of developing therapies that can actually reach patients.

Organoids & Disease Modelling

Organoid platforms are transforming how we model disease mechanisms, test therapies, and evaluate patient-specific responses. Their inclusion strengthens the symposium's translational continuum from model systems to intervention strategies.

Cell Therapy - Industry Updates

Industry perspectives are essential for understanding where the field is moving in terms of platforms, partnerships, and clinical momentum. This theme will help attendees connect academic innovation with real-world development pipelines.

Inherited Metabolic Disorders

Inherited metabolic disorders offer a compelling space for durable gene replacement, correction, and enzyme restoration strategies. The theme aligns closely with the symposium's emphasis on rare disease breakthroughs and therapeutic translation.

Musculoskeletal Disorders

Musculoskeletal applications bring regenerative medicine into areas such as bone repair, cartilage restoration, and muscle disease. This topic broadens the symposium by highlighting how cell therapies can address structural and functional recovery.

Dermatological Disorders

Skin disorders provide visible and clinically tractable settings for testing gene correction, graft engineering, and regenerative repair. This theme will showcase how dermatology continues to serve as an important proving ground for advanced therapies.

Non-Viral Nucleic Acid Delivery/mRNA Therapeutics

Non-viral delivery and mRNA platforms are opening faster, modular, and potentially safer paths for therapeutic development. Their presence in the symposium reflects the growing importance of delivery science across the cell and gene therapy landscape.

Industry Talks

Industry talks will bring direct insight into commercialization, platform strategy, and lessons from active development programs. These sessions will add practical context that complements the symposium's scientific and clinical discussions.
